Historical Note
The Battle of Rivoli, 14–15 January 1797, ended Austria’s fourth and final attempt to relieve the besieged fortress of Mantua. General Joubert held the plateau above the Adige with some 10,000 men against Alvinczy’s 28,000 until Bonaparte arrived with reinforcements, including Masséna’s division. The Austrian defeat cost over 14,000 killed, wounded, and captured. The victory secured French control of northern Italy and forced Austria to sue for peace.
